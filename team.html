Meet Our Team

Get to know the people supporting care at Oaks Psychology.

Julianne Randall

Owner / Therapist

Julianne Randall, LMSW, is the founder and owner of Oaks Psychology. She strives to create a warm, supportive space where individuals feel comfortable exploring challenges, building resilience, and moving toward lasting healing and growth.

Julianne Randall earned her Master of Social Work from Wayne State University and is the founder and owner of Oaks Psychology. She is passionate about creating a warm, supportive, and nonjudgmental space where individuals can feel comfortable exploring life's challenges and working toward meaningful change. Julianne works with adolescents and adults experiencing anxiety, depression, OCD, trauma, ADHD, life transitions, relationship concerns, and other emotional and behavioral challenges. She believes that therapy should be tailored to each person's unique needs and goals, helping individuals gain insight, build coping skills, and develop greater confidence in themselves and their lives. Her therapeutic approach is integrative and draws from Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), attachment-based interventions, mindfulness, and holistic perspectives that consider the connection between mind, body, and emotional well-being. Julianne values building strong therapeutic relationships and strives to help clients feel understood, supported, and empowered throughout their healing journey. She believes that healing happens through genuine connection and feels honored to walk alongside clients as they navigate challenges, discover strengths, and create lasting positive change.

Susan Sharpe

Therapist

Susan Sharpe is a warm and compassionate therapist who helps individuals feel supported, grounded, and confident as they navigate life's challenges.

Susan Sharpe is a warm and compassionate social worker who believes people do their best healing when they feel truly heard, supported, and not judged. She earned her Master of Social Work from Wayne State University and is passionate about helping clients feel more grounded and confident as they move through life’s challenges. Susan takes a down-to-earth, client-centered approach that focuses on each person’s strengths and lived experiences. She aims to create a safe, comfortable space where clients can show up exactly as they are and take things at a pace that feels right for them. She works with children, adolescents, and adults navigating anxiety, trauma, family stress, and life transitions. Susan integrates Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) and trauma-informed care in a practical, supportive way to help clients build coping skills that actually fit into everyday life. At her core, Susan’s goal is simple: to help people feel more understood, more steady, and more able to trust their own ability to grow and move forward.

Laura Clarkston

Therapist

Laura is an LLMSW who provides genuine, supportive, and individualized care for children, adolescents, adults, and families.

Laura is a Limited Licensed Master Social Worker (LLMSW) who believes therapy should feel genuine, supportive, and tailored to the individual. She understands that taking the first step toward support can be difficult and strives to create a welcoming environment where clients feel comfortable, respected, and understood. Laura works with children, adolescents, young adults, adults, and families navigating challenges such as anxiety, depression, life transitions, relationship concerns, parenting stress, emotional regulation, and self-esteem. Her goal is to help clients build insight, resilience, and practical tools that support meaningful and lasting change. Her approach is collaborative, strengths-based, and individualized to meet each client's unique needs. Laura utilizes evidence-based practices including Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT), Acceptance and Commitment Therapy (ACT), mindfulness, and play-based interventions. When working with children and adolescents, she incorporates play therapy techniques and creative approaches to support emotional expression, self-discovery, and skill-building. Prior to becoming a therapist, Laura worked in community-based settings supporting individuals and families, including those with intellectual and developmental disabilities. These experiences strengthened her commitment to meeting people where they are and recognizing the unique strengths each individual brings to therapy. As a former educator and parent, Laura understands the challenges of balancing life's many responsibilities, transitions, and expectations. Laura considers it a privilege to walk alongside clients through both challenges and growth. Her hope is that each person feels seen, supported, and empowered to move forward with greater confidence and resilience.

Chelsea

Billing Manager

Chelsea oversees billing operations and helps support the day-to-day administrative needs of Oaks Psychology.

Chelsea is the Billing and Operations Manager at Oaks Psychology and helps keep the practice running smoothly behind the scenes. She assists with billing, insurance questions, administrative needs, and many of the day-to-day operations that support both clients and clinicians. Chelsea understands that navigating insurance and paperwork can sometimes feel overwhelming.
